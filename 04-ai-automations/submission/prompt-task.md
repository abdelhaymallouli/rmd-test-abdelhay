# Part A — Prompt task

## AI tool used
Claude, Sonnet 4.6, free tier (claude.ai)

## Why I picked this tool
Claude Sonnet 4.6 follows multi-constraint instructions reliably and counts characters carefully — critical for H1 and meta length compliance without manual editing.

## Prompt I used

> You are an SEO copywriter. Atlas Coffee Tangier is a specialty coffee brand based in Tangier, Morocco. They source beans from across Africa and the Mediterranean, with careful single-origin curation and artisan roasting. Their signature blend is called "Bab el Bahr" (Gate of the Sea), a medium-dark roast with notes of dark chocolate, dried fig, and sea breeze.
>
> Write exactly 3 H1 + meta description variations for the product page of this signature blend. Target keyword: "specialty coffee tangier".
>
> Constraints:
> - Each H1: 50–65 characters (count carefully), reads like a real page heading, keyword appears naturally
> - Each meta description: 150–160 characters maximum, keyword appears naturally, compelling and specific
> - 3 meaningfully DIFFERENT angles: (1) provenance/origin story, (2) sensory/craft experience, (3) place/location identity
> - Do NOT make them near-identical paraphrases
>
> Output each variation with the character count for both H1 and meta.

## Raw output from the AI (all 3 variations, unedited)

1. **H1:** Where Africa Meets the Sea — Specialty Coffee Tangier
   **H1 chars:** 54
   **Meta:** Bab el Bahr is our signature blend — African beans roasted in Tangier, Morocco, delivering dark chocolate, dried fig, and a whisper of sea breeze. (152 chars)

2. **H1:** Specialty Coffee Tangier: Roasted to the Last Note
   **H1 chars:** 51
   **Meta:** Every cup of our specialty coffee tangier blend is a study in craft. Medium-dark roast, open-fire technique, notes that build and linger. Order now. (152 chars)

3. **H1:** Tangier in a Cup — Signature Specialty Coffee Blend
   **H1 chars:** 52
   **Meta:** This is what Tangier tastes like. Our specialty coffee Tangier blend carries the port city's salt air, market spice, and slow-roasted depth. (143 chars)

## Final pick + 2-line justification

**Picked variation: #1**
Variation 1 leads with a geographic story ("Where Africa Meets the Sea") that immediately positions the brand's unique origin story and differentiates it from generic specialty coffee. The meta is the most product-specific of the three — it names the blend, the origin, and three distinct tasting notes, giving Google and the reader enough concrete detail to click.
